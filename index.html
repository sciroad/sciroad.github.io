About

I'm a Computer Science MSc graduate from Yıldız Technical University and recipient of the YLSY Scholarship, Turkey's most prestigious award for doctoral study abroad.

My research is driven by a core conviction: intelligent coordination among specialized components outperforms monolithic scale. I believe efficiency and capability can advance together through thoughtful architectural design, with small specialized modules achieving strong performance through proper coordination.

I aim to apply these principles to multilingual NLP, where extending language models to thousands of languages demands resources most institutions cannot afford. My goal is to build systems where specialized components can be added or composed without retraining from scratch.

Selected Projects

Modular Symbolic Music Generation

MSc Thesis

Built a hierarchical compound architecture for symbolic music generation: VAE learns bar-level representations, Diffusion Model generates creative sequences, LSTM ensures long-term coherence. Demonstrated that specialized components outperform monolithic approaches.

50% fewer parameters 20% better on metrics Published

Vocal-to-Instrumental Transformation

BSc Thesis

Developed a system that automatically transcribes sung melodies into instrumental notation by extracting melodic contours and mapping to discrete notes. Self-taught audio signal processing to build the pipeline.

Minimum Energy Path Ensemble

Research

Investigated the optimal path between neural network optima in weight space and built ensembles by sampling along this path. Applied to image and text classification, demonstrating cross-domain versatility.
